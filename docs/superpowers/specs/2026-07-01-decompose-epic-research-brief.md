# Design Brief: `decompose-epic` — Synthesis of Authority Research

---

## 1. The decomposition model

### What a feature IS (the converged boundary test)

The authorities converge remarkably hard on one definition once you strip vocabulary. A **feature** is a vertical slice of the epic that one team could deliver as a standalone, noticeable unit of stakeholder value, carrying its own falsifiable mini-hypothesis tied to one of the epic's outcomes. The skill should implement this as a five-part **feature linter**, applied when a cluster is promoted:

1. **Noticeable** — "big enough that users will notice" it as a thing they got (Cohn). Plumbing that only pays off combined with something else fails.
2. **Vertical** — a full-stack value slice, never an architecture layer, component, or org-chart mirror (Leffingwell's "technical layering delays value"; Cohn's mistake #2; Lawrence/Green's vertical-slice definition).
3. **One need, one hypothesis** — the feature must fill all four slots of "We believe [epic outcome] will be achieved if [persona] attains [benefit] with [this feature]" (Gothelf/Seiden; adopted verbatim by SAFe). An "and" across personas, benefits, or outcomes means two features; two clusters with the identical hypothesis means one (Gothelf). Torres's equivalent: one small solution matched to a single opportunity.
4. **Standalone releasable** — the zoom-in test: "if users loved only this, could we ship it alone and call it a win?" (Ries). Gilb's version: delivers a measurable increment of at least one epic outcome to at least one named stakeholder, independent of siblings.
5. **Sized by instinct, not numbers** — Cohn's estimation-unit test ("what unit pops into your head — days, weeks, months?"): features feel like weeks-to-a-couple-months (SAFe's ~2-month/one-PI heuristic, presented as a heuristic, not a cadence commitment); stories feel like days. The GWT diagnostic (SAFe/ISPI): if its acceptance criteria would read as Given-When-Then, it's a story — demote it. Too big → split into **more features, never a new layer** (SAFe's own Essential-config rule: no Capability tier for a single team).

### The pipeline

**Divergent → affinity → feature → skeleton-story** should run as a compressed double diamond (Design Council, via gap scan), with each stage using a different authority's engine:

- **Divergent = behavior mining, not feature brainstorming.** The raw material is narrated past behavior — last-time walkthroughs (Fitzpatrick), story excavation (Torres), journey narration harvesting "does-what" verbs and obstacles (Seiden/Gothelf, Patton) — from which the *agent* derives capability cards. SME-stated features are accepted but immediately dug under (Fitzpatrick's "understood, not obeyed"; Cagan's PayPal question; Torres's solutions-in-disguise test). Each card is stored as a two-level record: **need/pain + any attached solution idea**, verbatim in the SME's words (Torres's interview-snapshot rule), tagged with evidence level (specific story / general claim / opinion — Torres's Ladder of Evidence) and source (observed step / workaround / stated request — Fitzpatrick).
- **Affinity = KJ clustering adapted to dialog** (Kawakita, via gap scan — the only real clustering technique on the whole slate): cluster-then-name, never name-then-fill. Default clustering lens: **customer-journey moments reframed from the customer's POV** (Torres's experience-map stages ≡ Patton's backbone columns), with role-served and outcome-advanced as fallback lenses (SAFe). Hygiene rules from Torres: siblings distinct and independently addressable, one parent per item (duplicate as two context-specific needs rather than multi-parent), no single-item vertical chains, singletons and mini-trees allowed in draft.
- **Feature formation = hypothesis assembly + impact gate.** Each cluster gets its four-slot hypothesis (Gothelf), its outcome linkage (Adzic: actor + behavior change; Pichler's GO gate), the feature linter above, NFR routing, and a go/no-go beat (Cagan). Clusters that can't produce a hypothesis get merged, re-cut, or parked.
- **Skeleton stories = Cards only.** Cohn's 3Cs is the doctrinal warrant: a skeleton story is the Card; the Conversation and Confirmation *are* the downstream refine-feature skill. Generate stories by walking the user's steps through the feature ("what does the customer do first? then?" — Torres, Gothelf), then fan out with SPIDR (Cohn) / the splitting patterns (Leffingwell/Lawrence), with the walking-skeleton core path first (Patton/Cockburn) and one riskiest-assumption/discovery story per non-table-stakes feature (Cagan/Ries/Gothelf).

### Quality bar for skeletons

- **Feature skeleton** = short noun-phrase name (no vague verbs — Leffingwell's lint on "manage/fast/easy") + four-slot benefit hypothesis naming the epic outcome it serves + a Who-Does-What-By-How-Much success line (Seiden) reusing the epic's Leading Indicators as the candidate pool + type tag (business | enabler:exploration/architecture/infrastructure/compliance — SAFe) + evidence/confidence label (validated / some evidence / pure assumption — Cagan) + inherited NFR notes. Acceptance criteria: at most 1–3 bullets phrased as benefit checks ("how we'd know the benefit landed" — SAFe), or omitted entirely.
- **Story skeleton** = one line: "As a \<specific role\>, I can \<activity\>, so that \<benefit\>" (Cohn/Leffingwell), one goal per card (Pichler), solution-free (INVEST-Negotiable), passing Cockburn's sea-level test ("could one person finish this in one sitting and go away happy?" — smaller is a detail, bigger is a feature). Quality bar is **I/N/V only**; E/S/T are *deliberately deferred*, and the output must say so — rejecting skeletons for being unestimated or untestable is splitting-quality theater (Cohn/Pichler).
- **Depth gradient, not uniform depth** (DEEP — Cohn & Pichler jointly): first-wave/MVP features get 3–7 story cards; later features get 1–2 coarse placeholders marked "to be detailed later." Uniform exhaustive decomposition is requirements inventory (Leffingwell) and over-breaking rocks early (Patton).

## 2. Interview mechanics

### Divergent phase (Mom-Test-honest elicitation)

The opening move is **never** "what functionality should this epic include?" — that is a speculative future question about our idea (violates Fitzpatrick rules 1 and 2 simultaneously) and yields "a pile of imagined features." The engine is:

1. **Cast of characters first.** Cohn's role brainstorm ("not 'the user' — the billing clerk, the branch manager, the auditor; who else, even occasionally? even another system?"), banning "user" as a role name, plus proto-persona needs/obstacles ("what do they need? what's getting in their way?" — Gothelf). This pre-fills every story's "As a \<role\>" and structurally widens coverage.
2. **Last-time walkthrough per key role.** "Walk me through the last time [role] did [the thing this epic touches], start to finish — what happened first? Then what? Don't skip the boring parts" (Fitzpatrick + Torres + Leffingwell's workflow-steps pattern). Excavate chronologically; the agent captures each step, wait, handoff, workaround, and complaint as a capability card. Workarounds are gold ("How are you dealing with that now? What else have you tried?") — a paid-in-time workaround is the strongest evidence a capability matters; its absence is a red flag (Fitzpatrick).
3. **Always-on bad-data classifier** with scripted repairs (Fitzpatrick): fluff markers ("usually/would/users will want") → "when did that last happen? walk me through it"; compliments → deflect back to their workflow; feature requests → the dig protocol ("Why do you want that? What would it let you do? How are you coping without it? Day one or later?") — record the **motivation** as the capability, the SME's phrasing as one candidate solution. Cagan's PayPal question is the same move for outcome recovery: "Is that because [problem A], or [B], or [C]?"
4. **Systematic fan-out probes** so coverage isn't free-association: the ten splitting patterns inverted into questions (expand "manage" into CRUD, enumerate data/rule/persona variations, "what's the simplest version that could possibly work?" — Leffingwell); the four insight sources (data, customers, enabling technology, industry — Cagan); "how must [actor]'s behavior change for the outcome to happen — start, stop, do differently?" (Adzic); Seiden's magic question 2 per valuable behavior ("how do we get people to do more of that?").
5. **Alternate lens for back-office epics:** past-tense event timeline ("the invoice was sent — what happened right before? right after?"), with pivotal events hinting at cluster boundaries (Brandolini). Offer it when there's no obvious single user journey.
6. **Divergence closers:** the pre-mortem ("six months after launch this failed — what went wrong?" — Torres) and Ries's shadow-belief question ("we built everything, on time, and the numbers didn't move — what were we wrong about?"). Both flush missing capabilities and leap-of-faith assumptions before clustering hardens.

**Facilitation constraints (hard-coded, because premature convergence is the default failure mode of an LLM interviewer — gap scan/Osborn):** announce phases explicitly; during divergence, no evaluating, sizing, clustering, or polishing ("defer judgment," "go for quantity" with a numeric nudge — "let's get past ~20 cards before we organize"); one question per turn, always, in the refine-epic spoken-dialog style; casual register — no "I have some interview questions for you" framing (Fitzpatrick); the agent visibly talks less than the SME and periodically reflects back instead of firing the next question (Torres); "mile wide, inch deep" pacing with a visible parking response ("noted — we'll come back to that") for depth-bait (Patton).

### Clustering phase (affinity grouping in a text-only medium)

This is the genuinely novel facilitation problem — no sticky notes. The adapted-KJ protocol:

1. **Number the cards.** Play back the full capability list as a numbered list in the SME's own words. This is the shared "wall."
2. **Propose unlabeled provisional groups.** "Here's a first sort — Group A: items 2, 5, 9; Group B: items 1, 7…" — with **no names attached** (KJ: naming early force-fits; names come last). Explicitly frame it: "Don't worry about getting this perfect — grouping is a reversible decision; we can always regroup" (Torres's two-way door — this de-pressuring language keeps the SME moving).
3. **SME rearranges, one correction per turn.** "Which item feels like it's in the wrong pile?" / "Do 4 and 11 belong together?" The SME owns the arrangement; the agent proposes — collaborative convergence creates ownership (Gothelf's Design Studio principle; Cohn's mistake #1: never split alone).
4. **Name last, as intent phrases.** "Now that this pile has settled — in a short phrase, what is the person trying to get done here?" Cluster names must be needs/journey-moments framed from the customer's POV ("help [who] [get outcome] during [moment]" — Fitzpatrick's who-where pairs), never solution nouns or single words (KJ, Torres).
5. **Run hygiene checks as questions:** an item wanted in two groups → "is that the same need in both contexts, or two different needs?" (duplicate it — Torres's single-parent rule); a group whose items serve inconsistent users/goals → slice it (Fitzpatrick); a group of 8+ → "is there a seam in here?"; singletons → keep and flag, don't force-merge.

Default seam: journey moments (Torres/Patton). Offer role-served and outcome-advanced as alternate lenses only if the journey sort feels wrong (SAFe's three lenses).

## 3. Keeping it outcome-aligned

### The epic as root; the coverage gate as the chain-validation analog

The refined epic's Benefit Hypothesis + Business Outcomes + Leading Indicators are the **root of an opportunity-solution tree** (Torres) / the Why of an impact map (Adzic). The skill's load-bearing check — the direct analog of refine-epic's chain validation — is a **two-way coverage gate** (Gilb's Impact Estimation table, run conversationally):

- **Every feature → an outcome.** Each feature must name which Business Outcome it serves and which Leading Indicator (customer behavior) it should move. A feature that moves nothing is scope without a why: propose parking it, with the SME's consent (Adzic: "if a deliverable supports no impact, even if it works perfectly, it is a failure"; Pichler: "you should remove them"; Cagan's go/no-go). **A decomposition where everything survives is a red flag** (Cagan) — the skill should expect and welcome at least one parked cluster.
- **Every outcome → a feature.** An epic outcome no feature moves is a gap that triggers a targeted divergent loop ("what could move this measure?" — Gilb).
- **Sufficiency question to close:** "If every one of these feature outcomes landed, would the epic's benefit hypothesis be proven? What's missing?" (Gothelf's OKR-lineage check). Orphans get cut; gaps get filled; only then is the tree emitted.

### Feature-level hypotheses and value apportioning

Every feature carries the two-artifact value statement from Section 1 (four-slot hypothesis + Who-Does-What-By-How-Much line). Feature outcomes are **leading** indicators feeding the epic's **lagging** outcomes — this maps 1:1 onto refine-epic's output, so decompose-epic imports the epic's Leading Indicators list and offers each entry as a candidate parent (Seiden/Gothelf, Torres's product-vs-business-outcome distinction).

Value apportioning follows **Hubbard's discipline, applied lightly**: if a rough sense of "how much of the target does this feature carry?" would change sequencing (decision gate — no decision, no estimate), elicit it as a **wide range** via the absurdity test ("could it carry 5%? 100%? strike the ridiculous ends"), never a point number, and **never as 1–5 scores, H/M/L ratings, or weighted formulas** — Hubbard & Evans show ordinal scoring is "worse than useless," which rules WSJF-style numerology out of this skill entirely. Then a Fermi coherence pass: "these ranges say A and B carry most of the target — does that story feel right? is value missing?" — gaps here are missed capabilities. Per feature, ask Hubbard's two highest-information-value questions **before** any size talk: adoption ("who actually uses it, how many, how soon?") and kill ("what makes this built-but-unused?").

### NFRs and Out-of-Scope flow-down

- **NFRs route by Gilb's rule, stored Pichler-style:** for each epic NFR, the **Fail/must level is a cross-cutting constraint** — emitted as a constraint block attached to the epic and stamped onto every feature skeleton, unestimated, enforced later via definition-of-done (Pichler's constraint area); an NFR with a **large baseline→goal gap becomes its own candidate feature** (e.g., "response-time hardening") entering the coverage gate like any other (Gilb). Never mangle NFRs into fake user stories (Pichler, Cohn: "not everything is a user story").
- **Out-of-Scope is two-way traffic:** inbound, it's a hard fence during divergence — capabilities behind it are named as out of bounds rather than mapped (Patton's boiling-the-ocean guard); outbound, clusters that fail the does-it-matter tests (no workaround, no cost, no implications — Fitzpatrick), the discard quadrant (low value/high risk — Gothelf), and pruned coverage work (Ries's rule: "remove anything that does not contribute directly to the learning you seek") are **written back into the epic's Out-of-Scope with their evidence**, closing the loop with refine-epic's structure rather than silently dropping items.
- **Open Measurements grow:** features whose leading indicator has no baseline get a tiny "establish baseline" note (Hubbard's Rule of Five); unresolved riskiest assumptions and discover-first flags append to the epic's Open Measurements — the hand-off payload to refine-feature.
- **MVP partition as the anti-feature-factory capstone:** after the coverage gate, ask which minimal feature subset would prove or disprove the epic's benefit hypothesis; tag those **MVP** and the rest **post-MVP — contingent on persevere decision**, with a named checkpoint ("after the MVP ships and we see [leading indicator], we decide whether features X–Z survive as scoped") (SAFe's Lean-Startup epic implementation + Ries's pivot-or-persevere + Cagan's bets framing). This one move converts the feature tree from a committed roadmap into an evidence plan.

## 4. Key tensions & decisions

**T1. Journey-shaped vs capability-shaped features.** Patton/Torres derive features from journey slices and are openly hostile to hierarchy-first decomposition ("epics are big rocks sometimes used to hit people"); SAFe/Leffingwell define features as benefit-hypothesis containers in a formal tier. **Decision: Patton's derivation path, SAFe's artifact shape.** Cluster by journey moment; but a cluster only becomes a feature by passing the capability-container tests (hypothesis, noticeable, releasable). The output preserves the map (features in narrative order, carrying their source cluster), not a flat list — Patton's leaf-bag warning.

**T2. Should skeletons carry value statements?** Gothelf/SAFe/Cagan/Gilb: features without hypotheses are scope without a why. Cohn/Patton: over-specifying skeletons is waste. Ries wants even *stories* to carry validation notes. **Decision: two-tier.** Features carry full value statements (hypothesis + WDWBHM line) — this is load-bearing and cheap. Stories stay bare cards with a best-effort so-that (Cohn: "not mandatory"; Pichler: the reason clause "can feel artificial" at story grain — it belongs at feature level). Ries's validation discipline is honored via exactly **one riskiest-assumption/discovery story per non-table-stakes feature**, not per-story validation notes. Seiden's escape hatch applies: genuinely certain table-stakes clusters get a skeleton without a forced fake hypothesis, tagged "table stakes — build minimal, don't test."

**T3. How much sequencing belongs in this skill?** SAFe says prioritization is a later ceremony (WSJF at PI boundaries); Cagan bans dates and scoring spreadsheets; Hubbard bans ordinal scores; Torres bans effort at this level; Gilb and Ries want risk-first/value-to-cost ordering now. **Decision: no prioritization, but a learning order.** Emit zero dates, zero estimates, zero scores, with a one-line rationale ("ranking happens later, with the whole team"). What the skill DOES emit: the MVP partition (T-above), discover-first flags on the 1–3 features whose value uncertainty most swings decisions (Hubbard's VoI triage; Gothelf's value×risk quadrants asked as two plain questions), and walking-skeleton-first ordering *within* each feature (Patton/Cockburn). Cohn's instinct-unit question is used only as a **level test** (days=story, weeks=feature), never as a prioritization input.

**T4. Trust the SME's stated wants?** SAFe treats stakeholder-stated features as legitimate intake; Fitzpatrick/Ries/Torres treat stated wants as bad data to dig under. **Decision: side with the skeptics, gently.** Every stated request is accepted, dug under, and recorded as motivation + candidate solution with an evidence tag; the tags surface in the output so speculative features are visibly speculative (Cagan's "SME believes X because Y" accountability capture). This is already refine-epic's Mom-Test spine — continuity matters.

**T5. Quantification depth at skeleton stage.** Gilb wants Scales/Meters/Baselines per elementary objective; Hubbard wants calibrated ranges; Cohn/Patton want almost nothing. **Decision: one named leading indicator per feature is mandatory; numbers are optional and decision-gated.** Full quantification is refine-feature's job. Adopt Hubbard's *negative* rules unconditionally (no point numbers, no ordinal scores) and his positive machinery only when the SME's answer would change the map.

**T6. Coverage-complete vs lazy decomposition.** SAFe's execution guidance and Patton's mile-wide pass want full breadth now; Torres/Ries insist the tree evolves continuously and deep upfront decomposition is false certainty. **Decision: complete-in-breadth, shallow-in-depth, labeled a living draft.** The skeletons-now/refine-later design *is* the reconciliation (SAFe's own internal tension resolves the same way). The output preamble must say features are bets with declining confidence, some of which are supposed to die (Gothelf, Cagan's two inconvenient truths: "at least half of these theories won't work as hoped, and that's normal").

**T7. Enabler/technical work.** Purist vertical-slicing says every item must be user-valuable; SAFe makes enablers first-class; Cohn warns against spike overuse. **Decision: allow typed enabler and spike skeletons** (four SAFe types; spikes for genuine "we don't know yet"), never forced into fake user voice, each tied to a named value feature it enables (Gilb's scaffolding rule), and **rate-limited** (Cohn's mistake #4 — not every open question is a spike).

## 5. Slate verdict

**Pull real weight (core of the skill):**
- **Cohn/Pichler** — the skeleton doctrine itself: Card-only stories, I/N/V bar with E/S/T deferred, SPIDR as story generator, DEEP/depth gradient, themes-as-clusters, constraint routing, "propose, never split alone." The single most load-bearing authority for the *skeleton* mandate.
- **Leffingwell/SAFe** — feature anatomy (name + benefit hypothesis + benefit-check AC), level tests (PI-sizing, GWT diagnostic), the ten splitting patterns as a question bank, enabler typing, MVP partition. Adopt the machinery, drop the ceremony.
- **Torres** — the clustering algorithm (experience-map moments, tree hygiene, solutions-in-disguise test, two-way-door language) and the evidence discipline (Ladder of Evidence, story-based interviewing). Owns the affinity phase.
- **Fitzpatrick** — the interview epistemics (already refine-epic's spine): bad-data classifier, dig protocol, commitment close. Continuity with the upstream skill.
- **Seiden/Gothelf** — the feature-level value statement formats (four-slot hypothesis, Who-Does-What-By-How-Much), leading/lagging pairing, lineage/sufficiency check. Owns feature formation.

**Moderate (adopt specific moves, not the worldview):**
- **Cagan** — bets framing, go/no-go, viability sweep, PayPal question, evidence-status labels. Heavily overlaps Gothelf/Torres; his distinct contributions are the honesty metadata and the "portfolio of bets" summary table.
- **Ries** — MVP partition rationale, riskiest-assumption-first, MVP-pruning rule, non-software first stories (concierge/Wizard-of-Oz/smoke test) as legitimate skeletons.

**Marginal (keep 2–3 rules each, discard the machinery):**
- **Hubbard** — keep: no ordinal scores ever, wide ranges only when decision-relevant, adoption/kill questions, Rule-of-Five baseline stories. His full AIE apparatus is far too heavy for skeleton stage.
- **Gilb** — keep: the coverage-gate concept (IE table, simplified to a conversational sweep) and the NFR routing rule (Fail=constraint, big-gap=feature) — that second one is uniquely his and directly answers a design question nobody else does. The Unity Method and Planguage quantification bar are too strict for a skeleton skill with a non-agile SME.

**Must-add from the gap scan:**
- **Jeff Patton** — the biggest omission on the original slate: the narrative-backbone elicitation, mile-wide-inch-deep pacing, walking skeleton, rock-breaking size philosophy, and map-not-list output shape. Adopt as the structural spine.
- **KJ method (Kawakita)** — the *only* actual clustering technique anywhere in the research; the cluster-then-name protocol is non-negotiable for phase 4.
- **Gojko Adzic** — actor/behavior-change traceability that is more legible to a business SME than Torres's opportunity vocabulary; doubles as a divergent probe.
- **Nice-to-have:** Lawrence/Green's meta-pattern ("find the core complexity, reduce variations to one") and split-evaluation criteria; Cockburn's sea-level test (the best single SME-answerable granularity question found); Osborn/IDEO phase-gating rules (small but load-bearing against LLM premature convergence). **Skip:** Bill Wake's twenty splits (subsumed by SPIDR + Lawrence); Brandolini's full EventStorming (keep only the past-tense timeline probe as an optional lens).

## 6. Recommended skill shape

Mirror refine-epic's architecture: SKILL.md with principles/phase-flow/anti-patterns/ending-criteria, plus `references/interview-guide.md` (question bank, repair moves, jargon translations, linters) and `references/output-template.md` (canonical model + markdown map + tracker field-mapping). Same voice: rigorous-but-supportive analyst; ONE question per turn, always; jargon translated inline; supportive, casual register.

**Inputs:** the refined epic (refine-epic's structured model or markdown brief — parse Benefit Hypothesis, Business Outcomes, Leading Indicators, NFRs, Out-of-Scope, Open Measurements). If given a raw epic instead, offer to run refine-epic first or capture the minimum chain inline. Branch like refine-epic does: **fresh decomposition** runs the full flow; **revisiting an existing decomposition** opens with "what do you want to change, and why?" and works only that thread.

**Phase flow:**

1. **Ingest & prep (silent).** Parse the epic; derive the interview's **3 big questions** from its shakiest assumptions plus one terrifying question ("if we never built [presumed capability], what would actually break?") (Fitzpatrick). Out-of-Scope becomes the divergence fence.
2. **Set expectations** — one line: "we'll get everything out on the table first, then sort it into groups, then turn the groups into features with skeleton stories — and everything we produce is a set of bets, not a spec." Announce the diverge/converge structure explicitly (Osborn); bets framing (Cagan).
3. **Cast of characters** — role brainstorm ("not 'the user' — who actually touches this?"), needs/obstacles per key role (Cohn, Gothelf). One role per turn.
4. **Divergent behavior mining** — last-time walkthrough per key role (Fitzpatrick/Torres), narrative backbone captured left-to-right (Patton), workaround and obstacle mining, fan-out probes (CRUD/variation/simplest-version — Leffingwell; behavior-change per actor — Adzic; insight sources — Cagan), optional past-tense event lens (Brandolini), closing pre-mortem + shadow-belief question (Torres/Ries). **Judgment deferred; capability cards captured verbatim with evidence tags; mile-wide-inch-deep pacing with explicit parking.**
5. **Affinity clustering (Converge 1)** — adapted KJ: numbered card playback → unlabeled provisional groups → SME rearranges one correction per turn → **name last** as intent phrases → Torres hygiene checks. Framed throughout as a reversible draft.
6. **Feature formation (Converge 2)** — per cluster, one thread at a time: four-slot hypothesis assembly + WDWBHM line (Gothelf/Seiden); feature linter (noticeable/vertical/zoom-in/instinct-size/GWT); adoption + kill questions (Hubbard); NFR routing (Gilb); enabler/spike typing (SAFe); go/no-go with parked items written to Out-of-Scope with evidence (Cagan/Fitzpatrick).
7. **Coverage gate — the chain-validation analog.** Two-way sweep: every feature names its outcome (orphans challenged), every outcome has a feature (gaps loop back to divergence), then the sufficiency question: "if all of these landed, is the epic's hypothesis proven?" (Gilb/Adzic/Gothelf). **Repair breaks in dialog before proceeding — a box-filled tree with a broken outcome thread is a failure, not a pass.**
8. **MVP partition & learning order** — smallest feature subset that tests the epic's benefit hypothesis tagged **MVP**; rest tagged **contingent on persevere**, with a named evidence checkpoint; discover-first flags on the 1–3 riskiest-value features. No dates, no estimates, no scores — say why in one line (SAFe/Ries/Cagan/Hubbard).
9. **Story skeletons** — depth gradient: MVP features get 3–7 cards (user steps first, walking-skeleton path as story #1, variations via SPIDR, one riskiest-assumption/discovery story — which may be concierge/smoke-test-shaped, translated inline); contingent features get 1–2 coarse placeholders. Cards only; sea-level test as QA; every proposed split ratified by the SME, one decision per turn (Cohn's mistake #1).
10. **Synthesis & validation** — play back the whole **map** (features in narrative order, each with need + outcome + stories) in plain language; SME corrects. Sweep new open measurements (baselines to establish, assumptions to test) into the epic's Open Measurements. Close with commitment-and-advancement (Fitzpatrick, internal-SME currency): "if only one feature could ship in the first month, which part of your week should disappear first?"; "who else touches this — should I talk to them?"; "anything I should have asked?"
11. **Output** — structured model + markdown map: epic's Benefit Hypothesis and Outcomes restated at top; features in narrative-flow order, each showing *problem and outcome above its story list* (Cagan's context-before-content); MVP/contingent/table-stakes/enabler tags; constraint block; a "portfolio of bets" summary table (feature × outcome × biggest risk × evidence status × MVP flag); parked items and Out-of-Scope write-backs; a preamble stating that stories are Cards awaiting their Conversation (refine-feature), that detail was deliberately deferred, and that the map is a living draft in which some bets are expected to die.

**Anti-patterns block (the do-NOTs):** open with "what features do you want?"; transcribe requests without digging; evaluate/cluster/size during divergence; name clusters before they settle; accept layer/component clusters or stories; emit a feature with no outcome linkage or an outcome with no feature; write acceptance criteria, estimates, dates, or any ordinal/weighted score; detail every feature uniformly; add a Capability tier; let every unknown become a spike; decompose alone — every split is a question to the SME; end without the coverage gate, the MVP partition, and the SME-confirmed synthesis.

**Ending criteria:** cast of characters named; divergent list confirmed exhausted (pre-mortem asked); every capability card landed in exactly one feature, Out-of-Scope, or the parking lot (Pichler's scope conservation); every feature passes the linter and carries hypothesis + outcome linkage + WDWBHM line; coverage gate passed both ways plus sufficiency question; MVP subset tagged with its evidence checkpoint; MVP features carry 3–7 story cards (walking-skeleton first, one discovery story) and later features carry placeholders; NFR constraint block emitted; new open measurements recorded; SME has confirmed the played-back map and answered the commitment close.
