# Dan Martell frameworks — bottleneck mapping cheat-sheet

Read this after you've diagnosed the root constraint (interview Stage 5). Use it to
name the bottleneck in the vocabulary Dan teaches, so the question lands in his
language and he can answer faster and deeper.

**Rule:** Use these framework *names and concepts* (they're public and from his
book/talks). Do **not** invent quotes from Dan or claim he said something specific.

## Quick map: symptom → likely root → Martell frame

| What the member says (symptom) | Likely root constraint | Frame it as… |
|---|---|---|
| "I'm slammed / no time / in the weeds" | Doing work below their rate; nothing delegated | **Buyback Rate + DRIP Matrix** |
| "My team keeps dumping decisions on me" | No decision-making system; founder is the answer key | **1-3-1 Rule** |
| "I can't hand things off / quality drops when I delegate" | No documented process | **Camcorder Method + 4 Cs + 10-80-10** |
| "Growth has stalled / plateaued" | Single fragile channel; no engine | **Growth engine: inbound / outbound / partners** (Phase 3) |
| "Everything runs through me / I'm the bottleneck on decisions" | Founder hasn't installed leadership | **"Fire yourself" / Install Leadership** (Phase 5) |
| "We're busy but not profitable / scattered offer" | Unclear, bloated offer | **Clarify the Offer** (Phase 2) |
| "First hire / I can't afford help" | Wrong first-hire mental model | **EA-first hiring / "buy back time, don't add headcount to grow"** |
| "I'm successful but miserable / want to sell" | Hit the Pain Line, choosing a wrong response | **The Pain Line + Buy Back Your Time** |

## The frameworks

### Buy Back Your Time (the core philosophy)
The governing idea: **don't hire to grow your business — hire to buy back your
time.** Every hour spent on low-value work is an hour not spent on the few things
only the founder can do. The goal archetype is the **Empire Builder** (buys back
time and reinvests it in growth), versus the operator who stays trapped in the
work.

### Buyback Rate
The number that tells you what your time is worth and what to offload.
**Formula:** (Annual income ÷ 2,000) ÷ 4. Example: $400K income → $200/hr →
Buyback Rate ≈ **$50/hr**. Delegate anything you could pay less than your Buyback
Rate to have done. Useful whenever the bottleneck is the founder personally doing
cheap work.

### The Buyback Loop / Time & Energy Audit
The diagnostic behind delegation. Track your calendar in **15-minute blocks for
two weeks**, then highlight tasks **red** (draining) and **green** (energizing).
Offload the red, low-value work first. Good frame when a member says "I'm busy"
but can't say *on what.*

### DRIP Matrix
Sorts tasks into four quadrants by value and energy:
- **D — Delegation:** low-value, draining, repetitive (admin, invoicing, inbox).
  Offload first.
- **R — Replacement:** high-skill but draining work the founder should hand off
  next (often sales, marketing, or fulfillment the founder clings to).
- **I — Investment:** activities that add capacity over time (training,
  relationships, health, systems).
- **P — Production:** the sweet spot — work that makes money *and* energizes you.
Most "I'm the only one who can do X" bottlenecks live in the **Replacement**
quadrant.

### The 1-3-1 Rule
A decision-making system that stops the team from dumping problems on the founder.
Anyone bringing a problem must bring: **1** clearly-defined problem, **3** viable
solutions, and **1** recommendation. Reframes "my team won't think for
themselves" as a missing operating rule rather than a people problem.

### Camcorder Method + the 4 Cs + 10-80-10 (playbooks / delegation)
How to make work transferable so quality doesn't drop on handoff.
- **Camcorder Method:** record your screen while doing the task and narrating your
  decisions — documentation with no extra writing.
- **The 4 Cs of a playbook:** **Camcorder** (record it), **Course** (the steps),
  **Cadence** (how often), **Checklist** (what must be true every time).
- **10-80-10 delegation:** founder owns the first **10%** (outcome + context), the
  delegate does the middle **80%**, founder does the final **10%** (review +
  refine). The classic fix for "quality drops when I delegate."

### The Six-Phase Scaling System ("fire yourself" path)
The staged path from founder-dependent chaos to a self-running company:
1. **Buy Back Your Time** — audit, Buyback Rate, delegate the red work.
2. **Clarify the Offer** — the overlap of what customers pay top dollar for, what
   the team does best, and what's most profitable. Cut the rest.
3. **Build the Growth Engine** — three channels in parallel: **inbound**
   (content), **outbound** (cold outreach), **partners** (other people's
   audiences).
4. **Systematize Delivery** — playbooks so fulfillment doesn't depend on the
   founder.
5. **Install Leadership** — "fire yourself" from being the decision bottleneck;
   hand decisions to leaders.
6. **Scale Culture** — "culture is the decisions made when you're not in the
   room": values, vision, people systems.
Locating which phase a member is stuck in often *is* the diagnosis.

### EA-first hiring
The first strategic hire for most founders is an **executive assistant**, to buy
back the most hours fastest. Counters the "I can't afford to hire help" frame —
the point of the hire is leverage and reclaimed time, not added cost.

### The Pain Line
As a company grows, pain (overwhelm, complexity) rises. At the **Pain Line**,
growth produces more pain than reward, and founders often make a wrong move —
stalling, self-sabotaging, or selling — instead of the right one: buying back
their time so they can keep scaling. Good frame for the member who is "successful
but miserable" or "thinking about selling because I'm fried."
